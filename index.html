Theoretical neuroscience

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Center for Theoretical Neuroscience, at the Zuckerman Institute of Columbia University, working with Ashok Litwin-Kumar.

I use recurrent neural network models to understand the structure and function of neural systems.

Research

My research asks how the architecture of neural circuits shapes the computations they can perform. I develop theoretical and data-driven models of recurrent neural networks, with a focus on connectome-constrained dynamics, interactions between brain regions, and flexible timing.

During my PhD with Srdjan Ostojic at the Ecole Normale Superieure, I studied how low-dimensional network structure and multiple neural populations shape recurrent dynamics. Before that, I trained in theoretical physics at the Universidad Autonoma de Madrid and in computational neuroscience at the Bernstein Center in Berlin.

Teaching

Since 2022, I have taught Advanced Topics in Computational Neuroscience at the Zuckerman Institute, Columbia University, first as course coordinator for two years and then as an instructor.

I previously taught Methods in Computational Neuroscience in the Cogmaster program at the Ecole Normale Superieure in 2018, 2019, and 2020.
